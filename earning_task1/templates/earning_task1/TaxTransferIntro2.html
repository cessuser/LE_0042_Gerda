After the outcome of the competition is revealed, you and the other participants will be asked to choose between two tax and transfer schemes for the decision group. In one scheme, everyone pays 20% of taxes over their earnings and each participant receives 100 tokens as a transfer. In another scheme, each participant pays 40% of taxes over their earnings and each receives 200 tokens.

If the participant with 400 tokens lost the competition, this participant will lose 40 tokens of their earnings before taxes.

The net income of each participant is, therefore, calculated as follows:

earnings - loss from competition (if applicable) - tax payment + transfers

Each participant will choose in private which scheme they want to apply to the group. The computer will then randomly select one of these decisions and apply it to all participants in the decision group. Each decision is equally likely to be drawn.

Two rounds per period will be randomly selected by the computer for computing payoffs. Each round is equally likely to be drawn, and your decisions and outcomes in one round will not affect your outcomes in any other round.

You will find out your payoff from Part 2 at the end of the experiment.
